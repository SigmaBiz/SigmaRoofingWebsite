🛠️ Final Instructions for Storm Landing Pages

🎯 Purpose:
Maintain two active, high-relevance storm landing pages:
- `/hail-damage`
- `/tornado-damage`

These pages should only display when tied to **real, confirmed storm events** in the **Oklahoma City metro**. Content should dynamically update based on verified phrases and user activity.

Match storm data and phrases to any of the following cities:

🧪 ✅ DATA REQUIREMENTS:

- All storm triggers must be verified using **NOAA** data only:
  - SPC/NWS storm reports
  - NCDC event archive
  - MRMS/MESH hail data (if used)
- Do **not** use HailTrace or similar sources to generate or trigger a landing page.

✅ EXCEPTION:
- For the **historical hail report section only** (see below), you may use other trusted sources **if NOAA response exceeds 3 seconds**.

---

🔁 ✅ PHRASE ROTATION LOGIC:

**"Rotation" means updating the phrase + content shown on the page**, not continuous reload or rebuild.

Rotation occurs:
1. **Once per day**, if no visits have occurred that day.
2. **Immediately**, if a new trending phrase with high volume and a NOAA-verified storm appears.

Pick a storm phrase that matches:
- High Google Trends volume
- A qualifying NOAA storm (within last 9 months)
- Hail size ≥ 2” or tornado confirmation

This ensures the page always displays **relevant, verifiable storm content** — no dead pages or false urgency.
