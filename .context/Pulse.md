isn't a cosmetic bug hunt, it's closing the last structural gap so the finishes can go on uniformly.

## The decision we're in the middle of
We first hand-patched one notch (push the wall back 3 ft, extend the wing roof to meet it). It worked — and we
**reverted it on purpose.** A one-off patch per corner is the wrong altitude; the right move is a **general rule** so
notches never recur and the finish system (fascia / soffit / recess) applies everywhere by the same logic. So Antonio
is defining the **wall / overhang rule set**:

1. A wall drops from its eave to the **first surface below it** — roof slope, wall, or ground — and stops there.
2. Outward wall normal **W**. A per-edge offset **U** that is *simultaneously* the overhang, the wall recess, and the
   underhang width: **U_e = 18"** (eaves), **U_r = 12"** (rakes). U > 0 ⇒ the wall recesses toward **−W** by U and the
   underhang reaches back U.
3. The **underhang** surface (soffit): horizontal at the eave, **following the slope at a rake**; normal points down.
4. **Prim-interface rule:** where two prims meet with different eave heights, the **lower** prim's roof runs *under*
   the higher prim's eave by that higher eave's U_e — giving the recessed wall a roof to land on (this is what kills
   the notch, generally).

Confirmed just now: **keep the dropped fascia** (the fascia is a real board, soffit sits below the eave — not flush),
and **rake underhangs follow the rake slope** (only eave underhangs are horizontal).

## Hard-won lessons (don't relearn these the hard way)
- **Max-of-tents is the law.** Roof = upper envelope of full prim tents; the host is never cut; an ext is deleted
  where the host is taller. Never resurrect a global lower-envelope-of-eaves (it cuts the host — tried, rejected).
- **Walls eat roofs.** Never bridge a height gap with a ramp — drop a vertical step-wall; a wall stays a wall. (The
  new wall-drop rule is the generalization of exactly this.)
- **Build order = hierarchy** (main → sub → ext); an ext needs its host first. A junction artifact = an *undefined
  connection*, not an engine bug — you're using the model past its limits; **define the connection.**
- **Ground proportions in real measurements early**, before adding prims, or a mismatch can't be diagnosed (concept
  error vs. scaling artifact).
- **Don't spiral:** if tempted to bend a core invariant to force an edge case, stop and surface it to Antonio. **Verify
  every claim with a Playwright screenshot** — don't declare victory, ask him to confirm.
- **Keep the dev server up** so every edit hot-reloads (Antonio never runs anything): `npm run dev`, port 3000.
