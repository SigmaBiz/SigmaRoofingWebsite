# GLOSSARY — plain-language terms (for Antonio)

The big picture doesn't need these; this is so the details don't lose you. One line each.
Written 2026-06-05. (Heads-up: the engine word **"prim/primitive"** and the persuasion word
**"primitive"** are the *same word in two different worlds* — see both sections.)

---

## 1. Strategy / persuasion (the "why" language)

- **ζ-framework (zeta) / Z₂ vs Z₁** — a sorting test for any choice. **Z₂ = "load-bearing"**: its
  effect is *guaranteed* (it won't fail under real conditions). **Z₁ = "fragile"**: works only in
  ideal conditions, or it's just decoration. We **keep Z₂, cut Z₁**.
- **Primitive (persuasion)** — a basic persuasion lever. We have four: **THREAT** (you're bleeding,
  a clock is running), **STATUS** (you're being seen / judged), **CONTRADICTION** ("your snapshot of
  reality is false"), **IRONY** ("your story / movie of reality is false").
- **LEVEL vs RATE** — LEVEL = where things *are* right now (a still photo). RATE = where they're
  *going* (the movie). Moving signals out-pull still ones. (THREAT & IRONY are rate; STATUS &
  CONTRADICTION are level.)
- **Lands / projects / inner product** — a message "lands" when it matches something the person
  *already carries inside*. "Inner product" is just the math word for "how much two things line up."
  You never *install* fear — you press a button that's already wired in.
- **Installed detector / matched filter** — that pre-wired button. Evolution put a threat-alarm in
  everyone's head; persuasion = hitting a *real* one.
- **Honesty floor** — the hard rule: the thing you point at must be **real**. A fake signal gets
  sniffed out and routed around (and here, can also be illegal). Truth isn't optional, it's load-bearing.
- **Care-density** — how much genuine care + competence the work radiates. It's the "this isn't a
  scam" signal. Our meticulous chalk ritual *is* care-density (not decoration — it does real work).
- **State B / spectacle** — showing off for its own sake. **Forbidden**, because "trying too hard"
  reads as scam.
- **Re-index (STATE → EVENT)** — switching what the homeowner measures by: from "do I *see* damage?"
  (a state) to "did a storm *hit* my address?" (an event). The whole pitch rides on this swap.
- **Inversion** — flipping the variable they're watching: "you're watching the shingles; watch the
  paperwork."
- **Named peril** — insurance term: a specific listed cause of damage (hail, wind). Either it struck
  or it didn't — that's the event.
- **The Silent Lien** — the door-doc's core threat: once a storm hits, the parties who quietly backed
  your roof (maker, installer, insurer) step back, and a clock starts. *(Stays OUT of the inspection
  copy — it installs later, in the ADJUSTMENT phase.)*
- **Avatar** — the specific customer we're aiming at. Here: the **"or else I would"** homeowner —
  fixable doubt ("I don't see a problem… or else I'd act").
- **Corrigible** — correctable. Their wrong belief can be fixed with evidence.
- **HAAG** — the recognized engineering standard for hail-damage inspection (the test-square method).
  Industry- and insurer-accepted — that's why citing it carries authority.
- **Newton's law of inertia** — a body at rest stays at rest until an outside force pushes it. We use
  it as the metaphor: the homeowner is "at rest"; the evidence is the force.

## 2. Camera / shot terms (the "how it looks" language)

- **Shot** — one camera setup: how close (size), from what height/tilt (angle), and how it moves.
- **Establishing shot** — the opening wide view that says "here's where we are."
- **Dolly in / out** — the whole camera glides toward / away from the subject. In = "lean in,
  something's coming."
- **Crane / jib** — camera on an arm that sweeps up and over (the big reveal-from-above).
- **Orbit / arc** — camera circles the subject (the heroic "show it off" move).
- **Pedestal** — camera rides straight up or down, like an elevator.
- **Pan / tilt** — camera stays put but swivels: **pan** = side to side, **tilt** = up and down.
- **Rack focus** — the focus shifts from a near thing to a far thing (or back), pulling your eye where
  we want it.
- **Close-up (CU) / Extreme close-up (ECU) / Macro** — progressively tighter framings; macro =
  pore-level detail.
- **Pull-back reveal** — start tight on one detail, then pull back to show the bigger picture (one
  bruise → "and there are nine of them").
- **Low angle / high angle** — camera looks **up at** the subject (= powerful) or **down at** it
  (= small).
- **Bird's-eye / overhead** — straight down from above; reads like a map.
- **Dutch / canted / roll** — tilted or spinning horizon = unease / chaos. **We reject these** — wrong
  feeling for a trust pitch.
- **Static / locked-off** — camera doesn't move at all (tripod). Reads as "evidence, documentation."
- **Bookend** — the same shot at the start and the end, so the ending lands with new meaning.

## 3. Under the hood — engine / web terms (skip unless curious)

- **Prim / primitive (engine)** — a roof *building-block shape*. Our roof = a few prims combined.
  *(Different from the persuasion "primitive" above — same word, unrelated meaning.)*
- **Facet** — one flat slope / face of the roof (the letters A–L on the plan view).
- **Facet plane** — the flat mathematical surface a slope sits on. We need it to "stick" the chalk
  square onto the slope at the right angle so it lies flush.
- **Normal** — the arrow pointing straight out of a surface (tells us which way a slope faces).
- **Centroid** — the center point of a shape (where we anchor a label or the test square).
- **In-plane basis** — the two "flat" directions that run along a slope; lets us lay marks on the
  slope so they sit flush instead of floating.
- **Keyframe** — a saved camera position at a specific scroll point. The engine smoothly travels
  between keyframes as you scroll. (Our cinematic = 6 keyframes.)
- **Interpolate / lerp** — smoothly blend between two saved values (e.g., glide from keyframe A to B).
- **up-vector** — which way the camera calls "up." Point it one way → north-up map; another → tilted 3-D.
- **Scroll-gated / scroll-driven** — tying what's visible to *how far you've scrolled*. Scroll = the
  remote control for the whole sequence.
- **HTML overlay (`<Html>`)** — 2-D text / graphics floated on top of the 3-D scene (our A–L labels,
  the text clouds, the hit count).
- **Render / Playwright** — Playwright is the robot that loads the page and snaps screenshots so I can
  *see* the scene and check my work without you having to.
